Resources: Collected Recources

About page sources

Mitnick, K. D., & Simon, W. L. (2002). The Art of Deception: Controlling the Human Element of Security. Wiley.
This book by Kevin Mitnick, one of the most notorious hackers of the 20th century, provides an in-depth look at the techniques of social engineering and how they have been used to exploit human trust.

Smith, S. W. (2021). The Craft of System Security. Pearson Education
This book provides historical context on the evolution of cybersecurity threats, including social engineering, and discusses how these threats have adapted with technological advancements.

Granger, S. (2001). Social Engineering Fundamentals, Part I: Hacker Tactics. SecurityFocus.
An article that explores the basics of social engineering and how it has evolved, providing a historical overview of common tactics used by attackers.

Symantec Corporation. (2010). The Evolution of Phishing Attacks: 1996–2010
A detailed report that tracks the development of phishing attacks over time, from early email scams to sophisticated spear-phishing campaigns.

Ashton, K. (2009). That 'Internet of Things' Thing. RFID Journal.
Kevin Ashton's article is one of the earliest and most influential discussions on the concept of IoT, providing insights into its origins and potential.

Mattern, F., & Floerkemeier, C. (2010). From the Internet of Computers to the Internet of Things. Informatik-Spektrum, 33(2), 107-121.
This paper explores the transition from traditional computing networks to the Internet of Things, discussing key technological milestones and challenges.

Gubbi, J., Buyya, R., Marusic, S., & Palaniswami, M. (2013). Internet of Things (IoT): A vision, architectural elements, and future directions. Future Generation Computer Systems, 29(7), 1645-1660.
This comprehensive overview provides a detailed analysis of the architectural elements and future directions of IoT, with a focus on how the technology has evolved.

Rose, K., Eldridge, S., & Chapin, L. (2015). The Internet of Things: An Overview. Internet Society.
This report by the Internet Society offers a broad overview of the IoT landscape, including its history, potential applications, and the challenges it faces.

Greengard, S. (2015). The Internet of Things. MIT Press.
This book offers an accessible introduction to IoT, covering its history, technology, and societal implications, making it an excellent resource for understanding the broader context of IoT.

Ethical Considerations page sources

Floridi, L., & Taddeo, M. (2016). The Ethics of Information Warfare. Springer.
This book provides a deep dive into the ethical issues surrounding information warfare, including social engineering, and offers frameworks for understanding the moral responsibilities of various stakeholders.

Brey, P. (2017). Ethical aspects of information security and privacy. Encyclopedia of Information Ethics and Security.
Brey's work discusses the ethical implications of information security, particularly in relation to privacy and the responsibilities of organizations in safeguarding data.

Nissenbaum, H. (2004). Privacy as contextual integrity. Washington Law Review, 79(1), 119-158.
This paper introduces the concept of "contextual integrity" in privacy, which is highly relevant to the discussion of ethical considerations in IoT security, where privacy and security must be balanced in context-specific ways.

Wright, D., & Friedewald, M. (2013). Integrating privacy and ethical impact assessments. Science and Public Policy, 40(6), 755-766.
This article explores the integration of ethical considerations into privacy impact assessments, a useful approach when evaluating the ethical implications of IoT systems and social engineering.

Floridi, L. (2013). The Ethics of Information. Oxford University Press.
Floridi’s book is a comprehensive resource on the ethics of information technology, providing a philosophical foundation for understanding the ethical challenges posed by social engineering and IoT security.

Security Measures page sources

Conti, M., Dehghantanha, A., Franke, K., & Watson, S. (2018). Internet of Things security and forensics: Challenges and opportunities. Future Generation Computer Systems, 78, 544-546.
This article discusses the security challenges in IoT devices, including common targets for hackers and the motivations behind these attacks.

Sicari, S., Rizzardi, A., Grieco, L. A., & Coen-Porisini, A. (2015). Security, privacy and trust in Internet of Things: The road ahead. Computer Networks, 76, 146-164.
This paper provides an overview of the main security issues in IoT, including the types of data and control mechanisms hackers commonly target.

Wortmann, F., & Flüchter, K. (2015). Internet of Things: Technology and value added. Business & Information Systems Engineering, 57(3), 221-224.
This source explains the value IoT brings to various industries and the associated risks, particularly the types of data hackers target and why.

Stojmenovic, I., & Wen, S. (2014). The fog computing paradigm: Scenarios and security issues. Proceedings of the 2014 Federated Conference on Computer Science and Information Systems, 1-8.
This paper touches on the security issues in fog and IoT computing, including how and why hackers target specific elements of IoT systems.

Security Measures page sources

Hickins, M. (2017). Casino Hacked Through Fish-Tank Thermometer. The Wall Street Journal.
This article details the 2017 casino hack through a smart fish tank, highlighting the vulnerabilities of IoT devices in corporate environments.

Greenberg, A. (2015). Hackers Remotely Kill a Jeep on the Highway—With Me in It. Wired.
A firsthand account of the Jeep Cherokee hack, providing insights into the vulnerabilities of connected vehicles.

Krebs, B. (2016). Krebs on Security. The Target Breach by the Numbers.
A detailed analysis of the Target data breach, including how social engineering and third-party access played a role in the attack.

Pagliery, J. (2014). Baby Monitor Hacked, Parents Terrified. CNN.
Coverage of the baby monitor hacking incident, emphasizing the risks of unsecured IoT devices in the home.
